## Strong findings suitable for planning

Subject to source capture and final verification, the report strongly supports these planning conclusions:

1. First-party owner-authorized data is the strongest evidence class.
2. Public accessibility does not imply unrestricted automation, storage, or reuse.
3. Logged-in marketplace dashboard scraping is high risk and should remain prohibited absent separate authority and legal review.
4. Etsy's current posture strongly supports retaining the existing no-scrape rule unless written authorization changes the boundary.
5. Shopify merchant and customer data may have express AI/ML and derived-use restrictions requiring explicit consent.
6. DataForSEO's public terms appear insufficiently explicit about long-term storage, redistribution, derived works, benchmarking, model calibration, and client-deliverable use.
7. Provider silence is not permission; written clarification is required for consequential reuse.
8. Cross-client learning requires purpose-specific contractual and provider rights.
9. AI/GEO scores without disclosed prompt sets, run counts, model versions, sampling methods, and calculations cannot support strong client claims.
10. First-party histories and point-in-time AI, SERP, and marketplace observations are perishable.
11. Marketplace competitor conversion and impression estimates should not be represented as facts.
12. Rights, retention, cost, and methodology must be first-class provenance for Observatory evidence.
